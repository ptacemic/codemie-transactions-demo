Section 1: Transaction Value Thresholds
1.1. All transactions above $10,000 must be flagged for compliance review.
1.2. Transactions above $25,000 require secondary approval from the compliance manager.
1.3. Daily transaction limits for individual accounts must not exceed $50,000 unless pre-approved by compliance.

Section 2: Jurisdictional Risk
2.1. Transactions involving counterparties from high-risk jurisdictions (as per the latest FATF list) require enhanced due diligence and must be reported to the compliance team.
2.2. Medium-risk jurisdictions require a risk assessment form to be completed and attached to the transaction record.

Section 3: Customer Identification
3.1. Any transaction with missing or incomplete customer identification information must be reported to the compliance officer within 24 hours.
3.2. Transactions for new customers (onboarded within the last 30 days) above $5,000 require additional verification.

Section 4: Transaction Type
4.1. Wire transfers above $15,000 must be flagged for review.
4.2. Cryptocurrency transactions above $2,000 require a source of funds declaration.

Section 5: Documentation and Reporting
5.1. All flagged transactions must be documented with the reason for flagging and the outcome of the review.
5.2. Compliance reviews must be completed within 48 hours of flagging.
5.3. A monthly summary of all flagged transactions must be submitted to the regulatory authority.

Section 6: Exceptions
6.1. Transactions between internal Fnality accounts are exempt from value thresholds but not from jurisdictional or customer identification requirements.
6.2. Pre-approved accounts may exceed daily limits if documented and authorized by compliance.
